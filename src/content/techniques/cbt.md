Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is one of the most thoroughly researched approaches in modern psychology. At its core, CBT rests on a deceptively simple insight: our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are all connected. When we learn to notice and gently challenge the unhelpful patterns in our thinking, we can change how we feel — and how we act.

**What this looks like in session:**

We'll slow down and examine the automatic thoughts that surface when you're stressed, anxious, or low. Together we'll ask: What's the evidence for this thought? Are there other ways to see this situation? What would I tell a friend who had this thought? Over time, this kind of honest inquiry creates real, lasting shifts — not just insight, but change you can feel.

CBT is structured and collaborative. Many clients appreciate having concrete tools they can practice between sessions. Think of it less like excavating the past and more like learning a new language for your inner life.

**Good for:** anxiety, depression, panic disorder, social anxiety, perfectionism, negative self-talk, insomnia (CBT-I)
