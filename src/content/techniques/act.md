---
title: Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
summary: Rather than fighting your inner experience, ACT helps you clarify what genuinely matters to you and build a life aligned with those values — even in the presence of difficult thoughts and feelings.
order: 4
---

ACT (pronounced like the word "act") starts from a radical premise: the problem isn't that you have difficult thoughts and feelings. The problem is the struggle against them — the enormous energy we spend trying to think our way out of anxiety, control our emotions, or avoid the situations that trigger us.

**The ACT framework:**

ACT works with six core processes: acceptance, defusion (seeing thoughts as thoughts, not facts), present-moment awareness, self-as-context (a stable sense of self that isn't threatened by changing thoughts and feelings), values, and committed action. Together, these skills build what ACT calls *psychological flexibility* — the ability to move toward what matters even when the inner weather is rough.

Practically, this might look like exploring what you care about most, noticing when a fear or self-critical story is running the show, and taking small, values-consistent steps even when anxiety or self-doubt would have you wait until you "feel ready."

ACT is warm, evidence-based, and often uses metaphor and experiential exercises rather than pure analysis. Many clients find it both grounding and genuinely liberating.

**Good for:** anxiety and chronic worry, depression, perfectionism, life transitions, meaning and purpose, relationship difficulties, avoidance patterns, chronic illness adjustment
