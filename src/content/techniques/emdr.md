title: EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
summary: A specialized, evidence-based therapy that helps the brain fully process traumatic memories and adverse experiences — reducing their emotional charge so they no longer run the show.

---

Some experiences leave a mark. They get "stuck" — the memory stays vivid, the body braces as if the danger were still present, and ordinary triggers can send us right back. EMDR was developed specifically to address this kind of unprocessed trauma, and decades of research confirm that it works.

**How it works:**

EMDR uses bilateral stimulation — most often following my fingers with your eyes, or gentle taps alternating left and right — while you briefly attend to a difficult memory. This process activates the brain's natural information-processing system, allowing stuck memories to be metabolized and stored in a less distressing way. Many clients describe the experience as the memory "losing its charge" — it becomes something that happened, rather than something that's still happening.

EMDR is not hypnosis. You remain fully present and in control throughout. We move at a pace that feels manageable, and we spend time on preparation and stabilization before processing any difficult material.

**Good for:** trauma and PTSD, childhood adverse experiences, grief and loss, phobias, single-incident trauma (accidents, medical events), anxiety with strong body-based activation
